learn about the league of legends

League of Legends is a team-based strategy game where two teams of five powerful champions face off to destroy the other’s base. Choose from over 140 champions to make epic plays, secure kills, and take down towers as you battle your way to victory.

Jump into battle, avoid counterattacks, inflict tremendous damage on your enemies, and lead your team to victory.

There’s a lot to learn about League, so we’ll start with the essentials. Explore the guide below to get the rundown on the most popular game mode.

(DESTROY THE BASE) The Nexus is the heart of both teams’ bases. Destroy the enemy’s Nexus first to win the game. (CLEAR THE PATH) Your team needs to clear at least one lane to get to the enemy Nexus. Blocking your path are defense structures called turrets and inhibitors. Each lane has three turrets and one inhibitor, and each Nexus is guarded by two turrets. (CHOOSE YOUR LANE) There are five positions that make up the recommended team comp for the game. Each lane lends itself to certain kinds of champions and roles—try them all or lock in to the lane that calls you.

Enjoy a variety of rankings

It is a game that affects the tier variation of summoners, divided into Iron, Bronze, Silver, Gold, Platinum, Diamond, Master, Grandmaster, Challenger and the terms of playing the game are 30 levels or higher and 20 champions that they own. The main purpose of the rank game is to raise the rank until the end of the season after being placed, and at the end of the season, they have to play again.

Placement games have been called "temporary rankings" since the 2019 season. The temporary rank is calculated to reflect last season's final ranking as the rank received in the first batch game. If a player receives a temporary rank and then turns the rank game, he or she wins about 60 LPs during the placement game and does not lose LPs in the event of defeat. There is no promotion match. Therefore, below the temporary rank, the rank will not be demoted during the batch game.
